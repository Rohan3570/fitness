People who have a high percentage of fat are more likely to be ill and have a higher death rate than lean people. Exercise and eating the right foods in the proper amounts can improve body composition. Body composition can be measured using an instrument called callipers, a specialized scale, or it can be calculated by using the body mass index (BMI) which uses height and weight to determine your BMI.

People with strength have fewer problems with backaches and can carry out their daily tasks efficiently. Examples of muscular strength include push-ups, weight lifting heavy weight with few repetitions, and pull-ups. Fitness testing will be measured by doing push-ups.

People with good muscular endurance are likely to have better posture, have fewer back problems, and be better able to resist fatigue than people who lack muscular endurance. You can improve muscular endurance by lifting weights with many repetitions or doing sit-ups. Measuring the number of sit-ups you can do correctly is used for fitness testing.

People with good flexibility have fewer sore and injured muscles. Stretching before and after activities will help to improve flexibility. The sit-and-reach and the trunk lift are two tests used to measure flexibility.

PROGRAM STRUCTURE

Beginner Intermediate Advanced

What will the program address

Develop motor skills, acquire necessary knowledge through physical activities and cultivate positive values and attitudes for the development of an active and healthy lifestyle.

Acquire good health, physical fitness and bodily coordination through participating regularly in physical activity.

Promote desirable moral behaviours, cooperation in communal life, ability to make decisions, and the appreciation of aesthetic movement.

Have basic competence and confidence to face different challenges.

The program is exclusively designed to:

Give all students, irrespective of their ability, an opportunity to participate in competitive Event through a safe and challenging environment.

Provide students a platform to showcase their abilities.

Learn about the spirit of healthy competition, respect, and sportsmanship

FITNESS ASSESSMENT

Health related Assessment:

Heart and lung endurance or cardiovascular, Endurance, Muscular strenght, Muscular endurance, Flexibility, and body composition.

Skill related Assessment:

Agility, Balance, Coordination, Power, Reaction, Speed

Fitness striker school program is well amalgamated with the regular school syllabus to give them a better cumulative learning.

Equipment: All equipment provided are age-appropriate and designed specifically for each activity. We will have special “Limited Spaces” activities for children/days where access to the playground is not possible. The equipment provided is as per international standards. We follow “equipment for all” policy – for instance 30 Footballs for 30 students - to get the maximum output, zero waste of time and no waiting for their turn.

Special Sports Day Preparation: by our team in conjunction with the school requirements.

Yoga Sessions: Our Yoga training program offers a unique holistic approach that specifically addresses the needs and capabilities of children to empower them.

Home Play Activities: The program helps promote physical activity outside the school and “into the home”. These activities are included in the FITNESS STRIKER @ Home Activity Tips’ and are taught to students by the FITNESS STRIKER PE Specialist to be done at home.
